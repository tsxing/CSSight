A Look at ChatGPT’s Simple, Effective UI

Tina Xing • January 12, 2025

Return to Insight

ChatGPT's interface is a prime example of flat design, a minimalist 2D style that contrasts with the earlier skeuomorphic design of user interfaces. Flat design avoids the use of color gradients, deep shadows, and "floating elements" in favor of clean, simple shapes. The only box shadow present in ChatGPT's interface is a subtle light grey shadow beneath the idea buttons on the "What can I help with?" page, barely noticeable. This restrained use of shadow exemplifies the platform’s commitment to simplicity, allowing users to focus entirely on their conversation with the bot without distractions.

A similar design philosophy is evident in other AI chatbots, such as Gemini. While they also embrace flat design, Gemini distinguishes itself with a pink-blue color gradient on its icon when the AI is generating a response. Flat design appears to be emerging as a hallmark of modern AI chatting applications, with its emphasis on clarity and ease of use.

However, there are a few design enhancements that could elevate ChatGPT's interface while preserving its minimalist charm:

Progress Bar: One possible addition is a grey or teal green progress bar to indicate when the AI is "thinking" of a response. Currently, a black circle appears next to the bot’s chat icon during this phase. A grey progress bar, placed near the input box, would subtly fit into the existing design. Alternatively, a teal green bar (or even a gradient from teal to a darker variation) could inject a playful touch into the interface, without disrupting its overall simplicity.
